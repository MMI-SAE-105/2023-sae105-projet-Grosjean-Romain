Steven Spielberg

Steven Spielberg, a rare and evolved director of our time

The John Hammond of an evolutionary journey through cinema

Written by GROSJEAN Romain

Published on 18 October 2023

Reading time: 2'min

When you think of historic directors who have significantly influenced the world of cinema, Steven Spielberg is a name that inevitably comes to mind. His impact on the industry is such that he could be compared to one of the characters in one of his films: the visionary John Hammond, who sought to redefine the way we perceive and interact with animals. In Spielberg's cinematic career, there is a sort of parallel between his work and the “fictional Jurassic Park”, where innovation and spectacle combine to create his entire fantastic filmic universe.

He made his debut with Jaws, the first blockbuster in history. From Close Encounters of the Third Kind to his latest blockbuster, Ready Player One... Spielberg has constantly pushed back the boundaries of narrative and visual effects in his productions. His ability to create thrilling stories that resonate with adults and children alike brings to mind Hammond's dream of a park where visitors of all ages can marvel at live dinosaurs as if they were his first attraction, his flea circus. A world where the effects are so real you can almost “see and touch them”. His filmography is a testament to his ability to move with the times while maintaining his distinctive identity.

Just as John Hammond's vision had its share of challenges, Spielberg's path to success has not been without obstacles. His ability to overcome adversity and continue to create films that captivate audiences reflects Hammond's unwavering commitment to his vision of a dinosaur themed theme park. Spielberg is a successful Hammond. Spielberg through his film has resurrected dinosaurs, breathed life into many genres and stories while evolving (Hollywood) cinema for generations to come, just as he did with his masterpiece.
