John Williams, a great soundtrack for a great film Harmonizing the mag/usic of Jurassic Park Written by GROSJEAN Romain Published on 18 October 2023 Reading time: 2'min In the world of film soundtrack composition and composer, John Williams is an almost legendary figure. As well known as Steven Spielberg, the composer with his exceptional talent for creating unforgettable tunes, drastically contributes to and enriches the quality of masterpieces of the 7ᵉ art. These masterpieces include his work on Jurassic Park, which gives even more consistency and life to the work as well as to the film park. These compositions have an unrivalled ability to convey strong impressions and emotions. They establish the atmosphere to fully immerse listeners in the cinematic experience being presented. His work on Jurassic Park is no exception. The musical construction creates a soundscape that sometimes reflects the greatness, the wonder and sometimes the primordial terror conveyed by the dinosaurs. This work should also be considered in conjunction with that of the film's sound engineers, who thought about the different noises and sounds produced by species that humans have never heard before. On this last point, I invite you to visit the “Palaeoart” section to find out more. The harmonious melodies, punctuated by moments of suspense, mystery and a certain majesty that the creatures already convey through their strangeness and sometimes titanic size... give the viewer the impression of being part of the adventure, and of not only “seeing” but “hearing” everything that happens. A veritable aural treasure trove, John Williams masterfully captures the essence of the story, giving the dinosaurs and their world a voice that resonated through cinemas and even years after the film's release. His contribution to Jurassic Park is not just a soundtrack, it is an integral and essential part of what made the film such a critical success.
