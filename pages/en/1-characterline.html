“Life always finds a way”

“Life finds a way”, a strong line with strong roo(u)t(e)s

Time to reveal the evolutionary power behind Jurassic Park's iconic quote

Written by GROSJEAN Romain

Published on 18 October 2023

Reading time: 2'min

In Steven Spielberg's 1993 film Jurassic Park, the character of Dr. Ian Malcolm utters a line that has become emblematic: “Life finds a way”. Although the line was not originally spoken in exactly this way, it is this simplification that has stood the test of time and resonates in viewers' memories. In itself, it sums up the very essence of the film from which it is taken. But what are these meanings?

First of all, it appears during a discussion that takes place in the incubator room. The subject of the discussion is the park's dinosaurs, and more specifically their reproduction. Hammond, the visionary behind the park, claims that they have genetically modified all the dinosaurs on the island to be exclusively female so that they are sterile and can never reproduce in the wild, without human control. Dr. Malcolm asks a question that's a bit hard-hitting: “But again, how do you know they're all female? Does somebody go out into the park and pull up the dinosaurs' skirts?” This remark, though expressed in the character's own humorous tone, touches on a deep theme of Jurassic Park, which is: the illusion of control.

Scientists and engineers like Wu and Hammond think they have dominated nature, but Malcolm reminds them that nature, despite all efforts to control it, always finds a way to express itself. It is unpredictable and defies our attempts to contain it. In essence, the quote emphasises this central key theme of the film and the saga, the consequences of human arrogance and our attempts to manipulate the natural world by playing God.

Although it may seem like an easy phrase, it carries an extremely profound philosophical message. It warns us by symbolising the struggle between human hubris and the indomitable forces of nature. In a way, it reminds us that, even now, it is vitally important to respect and preserve our natural environment to avoid further tragedies.
