Welcome to “Life finds a way”, a website inspired by an iconic line from the Jurassic Park film.

Enter a niche world centred around the franchise, dinosaurs and palaeoart!

“LIFE ALWAYS FINDS A WAY”

In Steven Spielberg's cult film Jurassic Park, Dr Ian Malcolm's line, “Life always finds a way”, sounds like a warning against the human arrogance of taking oneself for God in the face of nature.

STEVEN SPIELBERG

A renowned director who revolutionized Hollywood cinema, Steven Spielberg shares many similarities with one of the characters in his film Jurassic Park, John Hammond. Like Hammond, Spielberg has completely reinvented and redefined his field by combining innovation and spectacular.

JURASSIC PARK

Jurassic Park, Steven Spielberg's undisputed cinematic masterpiece, made cinema history by redefining our perception of dinosaurs. Released in 1993, it became an icon of popular culture, bringing “Dinomania” and “Palaeoart” to the fore.

“THE SCIENCE OF JURASSIC PARK”

“The science of Jurassic Park and The Lost World: How to make a dinosaur” explores the many scientific dimensions of the film and asks essential questions about the possibility and scientific reality of resurrecting extinct species.

AN ENIGMATIC, MENACING MELODY...

A powerful element in cinema, is music. Music is capable of evoking a wide range of emotions. In Jurassic Park, the music composed by John Williams is closely linked to the famous phrase “Life always finds a way” and adds significant depth to the story.

JOHN WILLIAMS

A living legend in the world of musical composition, John William's work on Jurassic Park has only enhanced the film's reputation and his own. His unforgettable compositions make a major contribution to the viewer's unique experience.

PALAEOART?

“Palaeoart” is a discipline that results from an extraordinary fusion of Art and Science, whose sole aim is to recreate extinct species with the precision and creativity found in other fields.
